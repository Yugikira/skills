# Compensation Benchmarking Network

## Definition
A directed network constructed from compensation peer group disclosures where firms are linked if one firm cites another as its compensation benchmarking peer. The network represents firms' relative positions in the managerial talent space.

Key features:
- **Spatial location**: Each firm assigned position based on aggregated benchmarking patterns (Hotelling-like talent space)
- **Nearby firms**: Demand similar mix of executive skills
- **Directed links**: A→B means A benchmarks against B

## Alternative Definitions
> How other papers define this concept differently

- Network analysis previously used for technological markets (Jaffe 1986) and product markets (Hoberg and Phillips 2016). Gao & Lu (2026) extend to managerial labor market.
