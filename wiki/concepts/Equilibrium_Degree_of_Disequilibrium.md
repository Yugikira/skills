# Equilibrium Degree of Disequilibrium

## Definition
The equilibrium fraction λ of informed traders that balances the value of private information against its cost. Prices partially—but not perfectly—reveal information at this equilibrium.

## Mathematical Representation
λ determined by γ(λ) = 1, where γ(λ) = e^ac × √(Var(u|θ)/Var(u|w_λ))

## Role in Model
The "degree of disequilibrium" reflects that prices never perfectly reflect information in equilibrium - if they did, no one would pay for information, breaking the equilibrium.

## Economic Consequences
- Markets are thinner when λ is near 0 or near 1 (beliefs become homogeneous)
- Informativeness ρ_θ² determined by n, a, c, NOT by λ directly
