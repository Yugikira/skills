# Analyst Resource Access

## Definition
The ability of financial analysts to obtain internal support (team members, research budgets, organizational backing) and external access (management meetings, site visits, industry connections) that facilitate research quality.

> **Key Paper**: [[source/summary/li_2026_allstar_award_affect_summary]] - Li et al. (2026) show All-Star award improves resource access.

## Alternative Definitions
Resource access encompasses both internal organizational support and external firm/management access.

| Definition | Source | Note |
|------------|--------|------|
| "All-Star titles unlock greater pool of resources within brokerage firms" | [[source/summary/li_2026_allstar_award_affect_summary]] | Internal resources |
| "Holding the title enables better communication with managers in covered companies" | [[source/summary/li_2026_allstar_award_affect_summary]] | External access |

## Related Theories
- Matthew Effect - accumulated advantage from success
- Halo Effect - prestigious titles confer access

## Determinants of Analyst Resource Access
Awards and status determine resource allocation.

| Determinant | Mechanism | Key Paper | Finding |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| All-Star Award | External recognition validates analyst value | [[source/summary/li_2026_allstar_award_affect_summary]] | +0.187 log team members |
| Brokerage Incentives | Retain stars, increase commissions | [[source/summary/li_2026_allstar_award_affect_summary]] | Brokerages allocate more support |

## Economic Consequences of Analyst Resource Access
Resource access enables higher quality research.

| Consequence | Mechanism | Key Paper | Finding |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Forecast Accuracy | Delegated routine work, focused expertise | [[source/summary/li_2026_allstar_award_affect_summary]] | Team members handle routine tasks |
| Management Access | Private information channels | Mayew 2008; Soltes 2014 | Better communication with covered firms |
