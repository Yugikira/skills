# Informationally Efficient Market

## Definition
A market where prices fully reflect all available information, such that no trader can earn excess returns by exploiting information that is already incorporated into prices.

## Key Insight
Grossman & Stiglitz (1980) prove such markets cannot exist when information acquisition is costly - the paradox creates non-existence of competitive equilibrium.

## Related Theories
- [[theories/Efficient_Market_Hypothesis]] - Fama (1970) formulation

## Economic Consequences
- If markets were perfectly informationally efficient, no one would acquire costly information
- Without information acquisition, prices would convey no information
- Creates fundamental conflict between efficiency and incentives
