# Analyst Industry Specialization

## Definition
The degree to which financial analysts concentrate their coverage and expertise on specific industries, affecting the depth of industry knowledge and quality of research.

> **Key Paper**: [[source/summary/li_2026_allstar_award_affect_summary]] - Li et al. (2026) show All-Star award induces industry specialization.

## Alternative Definitions
Specialization can be measured by number of industries covered or by concentration of coverage within specific sectors.

| Definition | Source | Note |
|------------|--------|------|
| "Industry knowledge is the most important factor in earning All-Star award" | Bradshaw 2012; Kadan et al. 2012 | Motivation for specialization |
| "Analysts reallocate efforts to focus on specific industries" | [[source/summary/li_2026_allstar_award_affect_summary]] | Post-award behavior change |

## Related Theories
- Industry Knowledge Theory - deep expertise improves research quality
- Effort Reallocation Theory - awards provide flexibility to specialize

## Determinants of Analyst Industry Specialization
Awards and feedback induce specialization.

| Determinant | Mechanism | Key Paper | Finding |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| All-Star Award | Feedback on industry focus value | [[source/summary/li_2026_allstar_award_affect_summary]] | 31.6% fewer industries post-award |
| Voting Structure | Industry-based voting categories | [[source/summary/li_2026_allstar_award_affect_summary]] | Awards based on industry rankings |

## Economic Consequences of Analyst Industry Specialization
Specialization affects research quality and investor satisfaction.

| Consequence | Mechanism | Key Paper | Finding |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Forecast Accuracy | Deep industry expertise | [[source/summary/li_2026_allstar_award_affect_summary]] | More firms per industry post-award |
| Investor Value | Timely feedback, industry knowledge | Groysberg et al. 2011 | Features institutional investors value most |
